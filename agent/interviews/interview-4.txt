Thanks for asking for honest feedback. Here's my candid take:

**1. Overall fun/engaging:** Mixed. The satirical tone (smiling-sun insurance company, DoorDash gigs, sovereign citizens) is clever and the stakes feel real—people can actually die. **2. Most confusing/frustrating moment:** The health recovery system. Health improved to "Poor" but never explained *why*—did resting help? Did the food help? Was it time? The causality was invisible, so I couldn't learn what actually moves the needle. **4. Repetitiveness:** River crossings follow the same loop (dangerous water → wait multiple days → water drops → cross). This happened 3+ times and became predictable. The "Press ENTER to continue" filler screens between real decisions outnumber actual choices. Some random event text feels generic.

**5. Difficulty/balance:** Oddly uneven. Early-game starvation hit hard—snacks ran out before the next store, suggesting the starting budget was a trap. Once I reached Buc-ee's with money, food became unlimited. Fuel feels abundant despite warnings. Money exists but hasn't created meaningful tradeoffs because I've had enough for everything that matters. The difficulty spiked early, then flattened.

**6. One thing to change:** Cut the waiting time at river crossings by 50–75%. Having me press ENTER 4–5 times per crossing, across multiple crossings, burns turns without meaningful choices. Either make water recede faster, add something *to do* during waits (mini-games, side events, trading), or add more crossing options that don't require just waiting. Right now it feels like padding.
